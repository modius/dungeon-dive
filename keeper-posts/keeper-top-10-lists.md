# The Lists

The Keeper has opinions about lists. They impose a false order on fundamentally unrankable things. They invite argument. They are, in short, irresistible — and the archive now holds twelve of them.

Daniel's ranking compulsion spans years and categories. The annual anticipation lists for [2019](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1391) and [2020](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1394) capture the optimism of a collector who hasn't yet learned what delays and disappointments await. The dungeon crawl rankings ([part one](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1397), [part two](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1396)) and adventure game countdowns ([part one](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1398), [part two](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1399)) attempt the impossible task of ordering the genre's finest. [DungeonQuest](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1389) earns its own dedicated review on the strength of a 15% survival rate and sheer elegance. And for those who think Daniel only stacks cardboard, his [top 10 horror books](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1395) proves the rot goes deeper than the table.

Elsewhere: the [top 10 "normie" games](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1390) for people who haven't yet fallen down the hole, [Tom Cohen's curated list](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1393) offering a second opinion, the [top 10 solo RPG things](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1400) for the happily antisocial, and [Forgotten Depths](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1392) — a solo dungeon crawl that earned its way onto the list by sheer ingenuity.
