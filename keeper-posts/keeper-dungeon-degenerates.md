# Dungeon Degenerates: The Hand of Doom

There are games one shelves with care, spine out, in the respectable section of the archive. And then there are games one shelves face-forward, daring visitors to flinch. Dungeon Degenerates belongs emphatically to the latter category.

Eleven entries have been filed — the complete degenerate dossier, if you will. It begins with Daniel's [introduction and setup](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1377), then plunges into a five-part campaign let's play as he attempts to [establish a base](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1375) in a world that actively resents his presence ([part 2](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1374), [part 3](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1381), [part 4](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1378), [part 5](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1376)). The game's distinctive aesthetic gets its due in a [discussion of the pulp art tradition](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1380) — equal parts underground comix, heavy metal album art, and the sort of thing found stapled to telephone poles outside punk venues. The [traditional review](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1373) renders its verdict.

The archive also holds a flip-through of [Die Wurst](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1382), the official zine (yes, the game has a zine — of course it does), Daniel's guide to [customising the game for sandbox play](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1383), and fresh off the table, a look at the new [Dirty Deeds expansion and Moon Madness](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1379).
