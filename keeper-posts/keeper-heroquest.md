# HeroQuest: The Obligatory Pilgrimage

One does not maintain a dungeon crawl archive without eventually confronting HeroQuest. The game that launched a thousand attics. The cardboard gateway that taught an entire generation that furniture could be threatening and that barbarians solve most problems.

Eight entries have been filed — beginning not with the famous original but with its stranger, more ambitious sibling. The [Advanced HeroQuest review](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1418) makes the case for Games Workshop's 1989 procedural dungeon system as something genuinely special, house rules and all. What follows is a complete let's play campaign: the [introductory setup](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1421), then four episodes of increasingly desperate Skaven hunting ([Part One](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1425), [Part Two](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1423), [Part Three](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1422)) culminating in [a finale](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1419) where the dice declare open war on the player. The subtitle — "Rolling All the Ones" — is not metaphorical.

Three years later, Daniel returns to HeroQuest proper with fresh eyes and no nostalgia to cloud the lens. [Episode 1](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1424) compares the 1989 original against the Hasbro reimagining, while [Episode 2](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1420) evaluates the new wave of expansions for solo viability.
