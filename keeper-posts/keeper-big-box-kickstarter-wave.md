# Crates of Ambition

Something in the receiving-dock changed this cycle. The usual parcels — a slim zine, a deck of cards, a paperback — gave way to *crates*: slab-sided things that took two talons to drag across the floor, each heavier than the game inside could justify, each rattling with plastic the archivist swore he never asked for. The Keeper knows this rattle. It is the sound of ambition shipped by the pallet.

These are the big-box Kickstarters — games backed in a moment of optimism three or four years before they arrived, the drowned worlds and haunted farmhouses and whole galaxies that promised fifty-hour epics and then sat, gleaming and enormous, on a shelf never built to hold them. Through every despatch runs the same quiet tension: the archivist loves the promise of the crate and distrusts its size. He wanted standees; they sent deluxe. He wanted a game for his table; they sent a Kallax cube. And still he unpacks each with genuine hope — because sometimes, under the wasted cardboard and the seam-lined dragons, there really is a world worth the wait.

Twelve crates. The Keeper has catalogued them all.

**Exhibit Catalogue** — the crates in the order they were promised, earliest first:

- [The Ghosts Betwixt - A Prototype Preview](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1941) — a family, not heroes, raids a haunted farmhouse; drawn tokens fix which ghoul hunts whom.
- [Assault on Doomrock - An Underrated Gem](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1945) — a tight-economy crawl so stressful he admires it more than he plays it.
- [Ghosts Betwixt - Unboxing](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1944) — household junk for weapons, and the finest index he's ever seen in a game.
- [Let's Take a Look at The Ghosts Betwixt: Chapter 1](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1940) — Saturday-morning art concealing an Advanced HeroQuest-grade crunch.
- [Stars of Akarios - A casual unboxing](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1946) — a whole galaxy he backed three years ago, and already suspects he'll sell.
- [10 Things I love about Stars of Akarios: A Great Game that's Not for Me](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1943) — ten things loved, one dealbreaker: the campaign that never returns to the table.
- [The Everrain - a detailed unboxing of the base Kickstarter pledge](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1938) — four years, a drowned world, and a neoprene mat printed the wrong size.
- [Valor & Villainy: Lludwig's Labyrinth - Detailed Unboxing](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1939) — he wanted standees; they buried him in free deluxe plastic instead.
- [Assault on Doomrock: Ultimate Edition - a detailed, casual unboxing and comparison](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1937) — a pulled card addressed head-on, then a roguelike layer that upgrades loot for keeps.
- [Dead Throne: World of Veles - a casual unboxing](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1947) — an open world that plays like it fell from a fifteen-year time warp.
- [Ultimate Tanares Adventures - a casual and detailed unboxing](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1936) — a box that fills a Kallax cube, saved for the group it demands.
- [Forging the Path to Arydia (unboxing, organizing, and the learning process)](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1942) — an RPG in a box, its whole story on cards, no campaign tome in sight.
