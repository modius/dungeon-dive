# Folklore the Affliction — Fall of the Spire

A bound dossier arrived this morning in two parts — a five-volume October-2020 campaign journal stitched into the cover of a later despatch from April 2024. The Keeper, who has handled such double-bound files before and knows them to belong almost always to games their owners did not quite manage to leave behind, files the parcel under *F* and reads on.

The series chronicles Daniel (@dungeondive)'s play-through of the *Fall of the Spire* expansion for **Folklore the Affliction** — three chapters of a Jack-the-Ripper-style hunt through Eürotrusk, undertaken with a *femme fatale* two-hero party (a dimension-crossed Druidus and a spirit-walking Huntress) and an unusual amount of house-ruling to make the system survive at two players. The arc closes four years later with a short-form video that points not at the expansion but at the *Adventure Creation Kit* book.

The frame Daniel lands on is that Folklore the Affliction feels to him like *a board-game version of a PlayStation-1-era Japanese RPG* — an overworld, small pre-rendered adventure tiles, a split between skirmish and map combat, item-and-class economies, fetch-quest rumours, and an enormous amount of bookkeeping. It is the only game he knows that scratches that particular nostalgia, and so he forgives the bookkeeping. The 2024 coda is a *love-letter coda* — the same shape as the Fallen Land run: this game stuck.
