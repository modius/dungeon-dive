# The October Annex

Every archive has a season when the lamps burn lower of their own accord. This cycle's crate arrived smelling of fallen leaves, old paperbacks, and faintly of cinema popcorn, and the Keeper knew before prising the lid: the October shelf had come due.

This is not a shelf of games — or not mostly. It is the record of the archivist's autumn rituals across three years: the horror novels read when the thunder obliged by rumbling on cue, the reading lists drawn up like battle plans each September, the top-ten films defended like castle walls, and one heroic October in which he watched thirty-one movies (the Keeper counted thirty-two; the archivist's arithmetic, like all the best hauntings, refuses to stay settled). There is one boxed confession in here worth the price of admission alone: a meditation on why cardboard struggles to frighten and pixels do not, recorded in a month when, by his own admission, hardly anyone was listening. The Keeper listens. The Keeper always listens.

A seventh parcel was promised — a 2020 reading list — but the courier reported the address no longer exists. A complaint has been filed with the void.
