# The Top 50

A six-volume folio arrived at the indexing wing this morning, parcelled in twine the colour of cheap dice. The cover sheet warns the reader that Daniel (@dungeondive) compiled the list in a single sitting in March 2022 — two hundred games dropped into the Pub Meeple comparison engine, the bottom hundred discarded on the way out, the top fifty served up ten at a time across five episodes plus a postmortem three months later.

The Keeper has previously had occasion to remark on Daniel's ranking compulsion. It returns here in its definitive form, spanning four decades from Talisman 2nd Edition to Mini Rogue. The rankings are honest about their own provisionality: the postmortem flags Tomb as a regret and names three games (Walking Dead: Here's Negan, Hand of Fate: Ordeals, Fallen Land) Daniel simply forgot to enter into the comparison.

Two threads run under the list. First, that *length* is the genre's recurring sin — game after game praised in the same breath as it is blamed for running too long. Second, that Secrets of the Lost Tomb beats Warhammer Quest at number one not because it is the better-designed game (it isn't), but because verve and pulp and audacity beat polish, every time. The postmortem's four negative reviews stake the same flag: balance, in this house, is another word for boring.
