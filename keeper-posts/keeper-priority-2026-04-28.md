# Two Despatches from the Solo Frontier

A courier from the Dungeon Dive arrived at the archive this week with two parcels and an apologetic shrug. *Priority,* he said, which here means: still warm. The dust of the season's planned excavations — League of Dungeoneers, the Cymbaline Isle expedition, the Top 50 retrospective — has been brushed aside on the lectern to make room for fresher pages. The shelves can wait. The Keeper, after all, takes new arrivals first.

Both parcels concern the solo traveller, though they could not be more differently dressed.

The headline is a guide — a proper guide, of the sort one keeps within reach for the rest of the year. [The Ultimate Guide to Solo Hexcrawls](https://dungeondive.quest/t/the-ultimate-guide-to-solo-hexcrawls-solo-rpgs-and-board-games/1557) is Daniel with twenty-odd hex crawls laid out on the table and the patience to walk through every one of them. He maps the entire genre across three terrains: the board games (HEXplore It, Runebound, Fallen Land 2nd Edition); the bridges between board game and RPG (Mieru with the Hex Kit, Legacy of Cthulhu); and the deeper RPG country, where Barbarian Prince's free-distributed patriarch still presides over the Drifter series, Mor'in, Wrath of the Wyvern, Forbidden Lands, the Black Oath catalogue, Grimscar, Kalaroth, and — inevitably, gloriously — Scarlet Heroes paired with the Sandbox Generator. The smaller end gets its due as well: 12 Years, Flying Ostriches and Floating Castles, Mark Cook's pocket zines, Pocket Hex, Songs and Sagas. Twenty-plus games, one definitive tour, the kind of episode you bookmark and return to when the wilderness needs naming.

The second parcel is a coda, slim and neon-stained. [CY_Korg](https://dungeondive.quest/t/cy-korg-a-simple-cyberpunk-solo-game/1558) is a cyberpunk micro-game in the MÖRK BORG-derived Korg line, where you are a gig worker hustling for a hundred credits to pay off a landlord who would let an earthquake topple your stack apartment without troubling to look up. Daniel takes us through a klepto run, an SOS rescue from a megacorp office, a cyber-doc extracted at the cost of most of his hit points and a glitched condition that follows him out the door. Coffee-break stuff, he says, and means it as praise. Some adventures are best at zine-thickness.

The Keeper finds these two together pleasing — one a survey of an entire subgenre, the other a single specimen pinned to a card. Atlas and ammonite. Both go on the solo shelf.

The planned excavations resume next week.
