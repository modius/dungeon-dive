# The Holy Grail, Cracked Open

A crate arrived this week wrapped in the particular reverence reserved for relics — for inside lay a true grail, a game so scarce (some two thousand pressed, late in the last century) that Daniel (@dungeondive) hunted it for years before prising a copy loose from the auction-houses. The Keeper, who knows the difference between a thing one *loves* and a thing one will *keep*, recognised the look in the chronicler's eye at once.

What the crate holds is the channel's very first Let's Play: a solo expedition through the cursed city of Burynathar, where two heroes — Bing the pyrotechnic and Dizzy the magi-duelist — wander its sprawling tile-laid avenues gathering clues. The arc is, by the chronicler's own cheerful admission, gloriously incomplete: no single plot solved across three hours, yet a wizard's war traced nearly to its end, an undecaying duelist's ring half-explained, and a phantasm of jealous love the Keeper has read three times for pleasure. The file closes with a verdict as honest as the archive owns — a poor game, a peerless work of interactive fiction.

The Keeper has shelved the run below in the order it was lived, with the deeper Warhammer Quest stacks folded in for those who would tour the whole world.
