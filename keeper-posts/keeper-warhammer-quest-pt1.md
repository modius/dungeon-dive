# Warhammer Quest: The Undisputed King (Part I)

There are dungeon crawlers, and then there is Warhammer Quest. Daniel called it the undisputed king in 2018, and the archive has no grounds for appeal. Ten entries have been filed — the first chapter of what may be the most exhaustive examination of a single dungeon crawl game ever committed to video.

It begins, as it must, with the [base game introduction](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1436) — a reverent unpacking of the 1995 Games Workshop release that set the standard everything else is measured against. The [Adventure and Role Play books](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1440) follow, revealing the game's split personality: one part board game, one part campaign RPG. The [treasure and event cards](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1441) round out the base game — the decks that give every dungeon its personality.

Then the expansions. The two big boxes — [Lair of the Orc Lord](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1437) and [Catacombs of Terror](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1435) — are examined in full. The warrior packs arrive in three waves: [Part A](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1438), [Part B](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1434), and [Part C](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1442), covering every officially released character expansion. And then Daniel opens the White Dwarf magazines — [Part 9](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1439) and [Part 10](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1443) — where the true obsessives begin to nod in recognition.
