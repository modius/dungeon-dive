# The Lost Parcel

Regular patrons will recall that when the October shelf was catalogued this cycle, one parcel was missing — a 2020 reading list the courier swore could not be delivered, the address having ceased to exist. The Keeper filed a complaint with the void. The Keeper wishes it noted, for the record, that complaints filed with the void are occasionally answered.

It arrived this evening, unmarked, slightly cold to the touch. Inside: the earliest of the archivist's October rituals, and arguably the most dangerous — five works of horror fiction to read before the nights draw in, plus a bonus volume for collectors of books about impossible books. Corporate demonology from Thomas Ligotti. Fifty miniature nightmares from a criminally unread Rikki Ducornet, two of them recited in full, which the Keeper considers an act of reckless generosity. A play in yellow that should not be read, reverently recommended. And two anthologies, one of which the archivist crowns the greatest of all time despite — or because of — its bargain-bin ubiquity.

With this, the October shelf is complete: seven exhibits spanning three autumns, filed and lamplit. The excavation now turns to newer strata.

**Exhibit Catalogue** — the final October exhibit, restored to its shelf:

- [Five (+ one) Killer Works of Horror to Read in October 2020](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1910) — Ligotti's haunted cubicles, Ducornet's nightmare miniatures, and a play that reads you back.
