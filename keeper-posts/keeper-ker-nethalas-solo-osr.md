# Lone Torchlight in the Deep

A heavy crate came down the back stair this week, and the Keeper knew its weight before lifting the lid: solo dungeon-delvers, the games a player faces alone, with nothing but a torch, a die, and their wits against the dark. Daniel (@dungeondive) has long held this corner of the hobby dearest, and here is a dossier on its OSR heart — anchored by the mega-crawl Ker Nethalas: Into the Midnight Throne, but ringed with the lighter, stranger company it keeps.

The thread is the lone lantern. These are not games of fellowship but of survival: roll-under skills and throttled damage in a living mega-dungeon; a rules-light system you bolt an oracle onto and house-rule the rest; a FromSoft-flavoured hex-crawl of undying bridge-warriors; a boxed crawl caught between board game and RPG. Some are slight, some are crushingly deep, but each asks the same question — how long can one torch hold back the deep?

The Keeper has shelved the lot below in the order they were written, the deeper solo-crawl stacks folded in for those who would delve further.
