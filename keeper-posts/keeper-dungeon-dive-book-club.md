# The Reading Room

There is a wing of this archive where the dice fall silent. The Keeper unlocked it this cycle to receive a crate smelling of old paperbacks and pipe smoke: the records of the Dungeon Dive Book Club — the season the archivist put down the cardboard entirely and picked up the books, then invited the whole village to read along.

What the shelves now hold is a complete institution: the founding charter, the monthly picks and their verdicts, an October tradition of devils and paperback curses, and the announcement of a nine-month pilgrimage to a certain Dark Tower whose echoes still ring elsewhere in these stacks. It ends, fittingly, with a book so treasured the archivist refuses to finish it. The Keeper understands this impulse better than most. Some volumes are not read; they are rationed.
