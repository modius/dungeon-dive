# The Shadowbrook Dossier — A Touch of Evil, Five Years of It

A damp parcel arrived at the archive overnight, smelling of sea-fog and lamp-oil, tied with what The Keeper suspects is fishing twine. Inside: four files bound in glossy cardstock of the kind Flying Frog used to print on, plus a fifth thinner sheaf already lodged in the *Adventure (Solo)* drawer last spring. The covering card, in the now-familiar hand of Daniel (@dungeondive), reads: *Shadowbrook, the whole investigation — base, Something Wicked, Coast, and the supplements that came after.*

The dossier covers five years of one game returning to Daniel's table. It opens in 2019 with the mid-game review — Flying Frog's possibly-best, "drowning in theme," a Hammer picture rendered as cardboard. It expands in early 2021 across three session reports, by which point *Coast* has come back into print, the Ghost Ship is anchored offshore, and five card supplements have been shuffled in. It closes in 2024 with a quiet single-mechanism dispatch on what still puts the box back on the table.

If the dossier has a unifying argument it is this: *the game writes the story, the player connects the dots.* The mystery deck, the minion chart, the lair cards, and the town-elder secrets cross-talk until a Hammer-Films plot arrives unbidden. The 2024 dispatch isolates the mechanism Daniel keeps returning for: the town elders. Some are evil, some are blackmailed, some are cowards — you find out by investigation, and the discovery reshapes the hunting party for the showdown.
