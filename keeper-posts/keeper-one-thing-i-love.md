# One thing I love about... — a 2024 micro-format experiment

A slim folder slipped onto the desk this morning, no twine, no seal — only a paperclip and a sticky note in Daniel (@dungeondive)'s hand reading *the One Thing wing, please file accordingly*. The Keeper, who has now been asked twice this month to start fresh sub-shelves on the strength of a hand-written paperclip note, files the parcel under *O* and reads on.

The series is not a series in the usual sense. It is a *format experiment*, conducted over nine weeks in spring 2024, in which Daniel set himself a single discipline: pick one game, pick *one* thing you love about it, defend that choice in five to ten minutes, leave the rest of the game uncovered. Five disparate games are catalogued here: a 1980 AD&D supplement Daniel was never allowed to own as a child, a 2024 zombie-survival RPG comp-copy from Free League, a Flying Frog gothic-horror perennial, a deliberately abrasive Mörk-Borg-styled parody RPG, and a co-op board game Daniel openly admits he discusses more than every other YouTube channel combined.

The frame that holds the five together is not a theme but a *discipline*. The form makes Daniel pick. Not the game. Not its strongest argument. The single most specific, most defensible *thing*. Across the run the answers are: a *chapter*. A *table*. A *map*. A *chapter of tables*. An *AI-controlled NPC*. The format suits him. The catalogue clerks suspect it suits the channel too.

**The Exhibit Catalogue:**

- [One thing I love about Deities & Demigods](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1642) — the 1980 Cthulhu Mythos chapter as a *historical document* from before the internet existed
- [One thing I love about this solo, zombie/survival RPG (The Walking Dead Universe)](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1643) — a d66 random locations table for a *mundane-modern* shelf that is otherwise sparse
- [One thing I love about...A Touch of Evil](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1644) — three small square pen-and-ink boards that read like maps from an old Hammer Horror film
- [A micro-review and one thing I love about...The True OSR](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1641) — Chapter 5's forty-plus d100 tables are worth the PDF; the system's adversarial GM frame may not be
- [One thing I love about...The Walking Dead: Here's Negan](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1645) — Negan himself as an AI-controlled NPC chaos engine, with a hidden reputation race underneath the co-op

Filed under *O* for One Thing, on a freshly-cleared shelf with room to grow.
