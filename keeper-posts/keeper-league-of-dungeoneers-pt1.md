# The League of Dungeoneers (Part I)

A crate arrived this week wrapped in oilcloth and bound with iron strapping, weighing — by the porters' weary estimate — close to thirty pounds. The shipping label was unfamiliar, the sender a Michael Dunsted of distant repute, and when the lid finally yielded the archive's lectern was forced to make room for what spilled out: tile-maps in two terrains, a folded hex map of an unfamiliar kingdom, a leather coin-purse, three hundred and eighty hand-drawn standees, and four hardbound volumes whose spines were still stiff from the press.

The dossier on the box reads: *League of Dungeoneers*. The Keeper has set aside an entire shelf.

The provenance begins, as so many of these things do, with a prototype. [A Look at League of Dungeoneers](https://dungeondive.quest/t/a-look-at-league-of-dungeoneers/1559) records Daniel's first encounter with the unfinished version in early 2022 — a game pitched openly as the meeting place of Warhammer Quest, Advanced HeroQuest, and Dungeon Universalis. Tile-built dungeons, a tactical combat system, an overland hex map, and most unusually a working sanity-and-trauma system for heroes who survive too many crypts. He pronounced it a no-brainer back. The Kickstarter agreed.

A year passes, and [the all-in unboxing](https://dungeondive.quest/t/league-of-dungeoneers-a-detailed-unboxing-of-the-all-in-kickstarter/1562) marks the crate's arrival from the Chinese factory, ahead of general fulfilment. Daniel rates the standees among the finest he has owned — better than the Pathfinder Pawns, even — and notes with quiet astonishment that the game shipped on schedule, a rarity in our trade.

Then the contents are unpacked, system by system. [The Companions, hero standees, and bestiary](https://dungeondive.quest/t/league-of-dungeoneers-companions-hero-standees-and-bestiary/1563) cover the hireable mercenaries who spare you the bookkeeping of a full party of four, the seven-faction monster catalogue, and a charming proposed house rule: anchor each named mercenary to a home settlement, so that hiring Three-Fingered Ali means travelling to the place she lives. [Questing](https://dungeondive.quest/t/league-of-dungeoneers-questing-and-creating-your-own-persistent-world/1564) reframes the entire game: not a campaign-driven epic but a sandbox of fifty quests, sword-and-sorcery short stories rather than fantasy novels, with The Dead Rising and the Halls of the Goblin King and the Cult of the Hydra and the Dark Pyramid all available in any order.

[Character creation](https://dungeondive.quest/t/league-of-dungeoneers-character-and-party-creation/1560) is where the Keeper raises an eyebrow. Combat skill is derived from Dexterity, not Strength — meaning Daniel's dwarf warrior with a 48 Strength still hits at thirty-five percent. He flags it. The errata accumulate. The bones, however, are sound. Bella Starkweather and Carrick Thorntok join the party.

Finally, [the game flow itself](https://dungeondive.quest/t/league-of-dungeoneers-the-lowdown-on-the-game-flow/1561) — settlement, skirmish, supplies, quest, hex-map travel with random events, exploration deck, threat dice, locked doors, wandering monsters creeping inward and rewarding the heroes who close the doors behind them.

Six volumes catalogued. The dungeon proper is opened in Part II.
