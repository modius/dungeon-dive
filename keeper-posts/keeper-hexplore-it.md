# HEXplore It

Some entries arrive in the archive as neat, settled things — a review, a verdict, a number out of ten. HEXplore It is not one of those entries. It arrived as an argument Daniel was having with himself, and the archive has faithfully recorded every round.

It begins with [an evolved opinion](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1411) — a public recantation, no less, followed almost immediately by [an addendum](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1410) to the recantation. Then comes a meditation on [the sheer volume of meaningful choices](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1407) the system throws at you each turn, which the Keeper suspects is the precise moment infatuation set in. [The Sands of Shurax](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1405) introduced complications — literally — and Daniel's preliminary thoughts suggest a man wrestling with whether complexity is a feature or a condition. [Valley of the Dead King](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1413) brought clarity, or at least a preference.

The Domain of Mirza Noctis warranted three entries unto itself: a [lengthy unboxing](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1409), [initial thoughts on the dungeon diving](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1408), and a session report titled, memorably, "[Blood? Blood!](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1406)" The [comprehensive guide](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1412) attempts to impose order on the whole sprawling series, and [Klik's Madness](https://dungeondive.quest/t/1414) proves the hex-crawl shows no signs of stopping.
